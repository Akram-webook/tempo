# ADR 0006 — Staged waves rollout + human merge gate

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-02

## Context
Tempo is an operations tool people rely on; a bad drop erodes trust faster than a slow one builds
it. The Release-Management discipline says ship small Operations Waves — one primary improvement
per drop — and observe before the next. Work is often produced by AI builders, which makes an
explicit human gate before anything reaches `main` non-negotiable. (Substance:
`ai-os/08-release-management/RELEASE-STRATEGY.md`, `docs/ROADMAP-waves.md`, `CLAUDE.md` golden
rules.)

## Decision
We roll out in **staged waves** (Wave 3.001, 3.002, …), one primary feature per wave plus polish,
QA, and stated known-limits. Every change lands on a branch + PR. **No AI auto-merge** — a human
(Akram) holds the merge gate to `main`. And **never hand-edit `dist/`**: the bundle is generated by
`build.js` from `src/**`, so it stays in sync with source.

## Consequences
- Small, observable drops; regressions are caught at wave scope, not in a big-bang release.
- The human gate keeps accountability for what ships with a person, not an agent.
- `dist/` is always reproducible from source — no divergence between shipped and reviewed code.
- Cost: throughput is deliberately bounded by the review gate and one-feature-per-wave cadence;
  that is the intended trade — quality over speed.
