# What to do FIRST — and how (researched)

> Question: out of everything, what's the first move and how do we do it? Answer below is
> grounded in adoption + motivation research (sources at the end). All app data is mocked.

## The first move (one line)
**Run a small, opt-in 2-week pilot on ONE team (yours), with a visible senior sponsor who
also uses it — and give every person one effortless "aha": their own "My Progress" page.**

Why this and not "build everything":
- **Sponsorship is the #1 predictor of adoption** (Prosci) — a leader who *visibly uses it*,
  not just approves it. Effective sponsor → ~79% success vs ~27% without.
- **Spread by respected peers, not by mandate** (Rogers' diffusion) — pilot with opinion-leaders.
- **Make it effortless + show personal value fast** (TAM, ADKAR) — if people don't see what's
  in it *for them* in the first days, more training won't save it.

## The "aha": the My Progress dashboard (built — 🎯 in the header)
Built on the strongest evidence we found:
- **Progress Principle (Amabile, HBR):** seeing daily progress in meaningful work is the #1
  motivator — stronger than incentives. → the **"Done today"** feed is the hero.
- **Endowed progress:** goal bars start already partly full (you're "6/8", never 0) → people
  finish more.
- **Gentle streak + freezes:** a habit nudge, never a red "you failed" — setbacks hurt 2–3×
  more than wins help, so we never shame.
- **Recognition/kudos:** the documented bridge to our goal — recognized people are ~half as
  likely to burn out (Gallup/Workhuman).
- **Self-referenced growth (level):** you vs your past — never a leaderboard.

## The healthy-engagement rule (so it doesn't backfire)
We make people want to come back for **progress + recognition**, NOT for grinding. The tool
measures **capacity, not hours**. We deliberately excluded leaderboards, public ranking,
shaming, surveillance, and anything tied to pay — all documented to cause gaming, anxiety, and
burnout (the exact thing this tool prevents). Slack's own rule applies: notifications "help the
user, not the company." Pass the **Regret Test** before any nudge ("would they regret this at 9pm?").

## The 2-week pilot plan (how)
**Week 0 — set up (1 day):**
- Sponsor: get Ahmed (or Motaa) to say "I'm using this" and to open it in front of the team.
- Add a **Size** column to your existing Slack List (S/M/L) — the one field that makes load real.
- Pick the pilot = your team (Akram + Idris, Gamal, Osama).

**Week 1 — effortless habit:**
- One gentle daily Slack nudge → people log what they got done (or react ✅). 60 seconds.
- Everyone opens 🎯 My Progress once a day. Managers send 1–2 kudos.

**Week 2 — prove value:**
- You (manager) do the weekly "look + rebalance once" (ease the overloaded person).
- Capture the story: "we saw X at 85%, eased it, they finished faster and didn't burn out."
- Show the team their own progress/kudos → that's the adoption pull.

**Success = ** people open it without being told + one real rebalance + one prevented overload.
Then expand to a second team via the people who liked it (not by mandate).
