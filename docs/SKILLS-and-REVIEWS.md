# Skills, Competencies & Quarterly Reviews — research + what we built

How the new profile sections (skills, EQ, manager/director lenses, quarterly
review, promotion-readiness, retention risk) are grounded in best practice.
Sources at the end. All data in the prototype is mocked.

## 1. Skills on one 0–5 independence ladder
Best practice (Dreyfus model + vendor consensus): rate every skill on a single
scale anchored to **how independently the person performs**, not vague stars:

`0 None · 1 Novice · 2 Beginner (supervised) · 3 Intermediate (independent) · 4 Advanced · 5 Expert (mentors others)`

Every skill is tagged **Technical (hard)** — reporting, English, budgeting, AV,
tools — or **Behavioral (soft)** — communication, ownership, problem-solving.
Each skill carries a **target level** (the grey marker on the bar) and a
**quarter history**, so the profile shows a per-skill **trend arrow** (▲/▼ vs last
quarter). The load-bearing number is the gap: target − current.

> So your examples map cleanly: "good at reports" → Reporting 4/5 (target 3),
> "good at English" → English 4/5. Both are Technical skills with a trend line.

## 2. Emotional intelligence — included, but carefully
EQ is **scientifically contested** (no agreed single construct; self-report
measures overlap heavily with personality; Goleman's model is often called pop
psychology). So we follow the defensible path: EQ = **four observable behavioral
domains** (self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship
management), framed as **development-only**, with an on-screen disclaimer, and
**never a gate for pay or promotion**. That's how it earns a place without being
pseudo-science. (This covers "his emotional intelligence, his relationships, where
his strength lies" — shown as behaviors, not a verdict.)

## 3. Two distinct lenses: manager vs director
The value is the **difference**, not duplication:
- **Manager's lens** = day-to-day execution & behavior, strengths, growth, a coaching suggestion.
- **Director's view** = strategic impact + potential (High/Medium) + a senior suggestion.

This mirrors the **9-box** (performance × potential). When they disagree, the fix is
a **calibration conversation**, not a fixed numeric weight — so the tool shows both,
it doesn't average them. (Skip-level/director feedback best practice: themes only,
listening not policing, always redirect to the manager.)

## 4. Quarterly review — cadence & contents
Annual-only reviews are discredited; continuous/quarterly check-ins lift engagement.
Each quarterly card re-rates the **same** skills against a fixed rubric and answers
the key question: **"did they improve on last quarter's focus areas?"** — shown as
"Improved this quarter" vs "Focus next quarter," plus a rating
(Exceeds / Meets / Developing).

## 5. Attendance/commitment — kept in a SEPARATE lane (important)
You asked about "commitment to work hours / outside hours / app usage." Best
practice is explicit: **do not blend attendance/conduct into the skills or growth
score.** It conflates reliability with capability, shifts the tone to surveillance,
and carries fairness/legal risk. So the prototype puts it in its own clearly-labeled
**"Reliability & conduct (separate lane)"** block inside the quarterly review —
visible to the manager/director as context, but never mixed into the skill ratings
or the promotion signal. (Exception: roles where presence *is* the deliverable — not
the case for this knowledge/ops team.)

## 6. Promotion-readiness & retention risk (the signals you asked for)
- **Promotion-readiness** = handles **Tier-1** work + **delivers** + **sustainably**
  (not chronically red) + **over time**. Shown as a % ring with a fairness note. It is
  a *signal a human reviews*, never auto-applied to pay (Goodhart's Law).
- **Fairness check** — if someone hasn't been *given* a Tier-1 in 4+ months, the
  signal says so ("give a fair shot before judging readiness") so the available person
  gets the opportunity instead of being quietly passed over.
- **Retention risk** — stalled 16–36 months in role + no recent promotion/raise +
  under pressure → an early "intervene now" flag. Directly serves the founding goal:
  prevent the next resignation. (Omar in the demo: 91% ready *and* a retention risk.)
- **Ramp-up** — new hires (Layla) get a lighter ceiling and a "ramping up" badge, so
  managers don't over-load someone still onboarding (~3–8 months to full productivity).
