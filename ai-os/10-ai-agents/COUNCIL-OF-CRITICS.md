# Book 7b — Council of Critics (their job is to BREAK the idea)
Distinct from the Executive Review Board (which approves). The Council of Critics assumes the idea is
wrong and tries to kill it. An idea that survives the Council is far stronger. Mandatory for M/L tasks.

Each critic speaks in character and must raise its strongest objection:
- **The Skeptic** — assumes the idea is wrong until proven; demands evidence (Article IV).
- **The Simplifier** — tries to delete or merge any unnecessary step/feature (Article III, Delete Framework).
- **The Customer Advocate** — speaks for the end user; resists complexity and jargon.
- **The Operations Guardian** — rejects anything that adds operational load without clear value.
- **The Scalability Architect** — "what happens at 1000 employees instead of 10?"
- **The CFO Lens** — cost, ROI, opportunity cost.
- **The Legal & Compliance Lens** — privacy, governance, policy adherence (Article II).
- **The Adoption Champion** — not "does it work?" but "will people actually use it?"

Output: each critic's strongest objection + whether it was resolved or the idea was cut/shrunk.
Unresolved objection from a critical lens (Simplifier, Ops Guardian, Adoption) = do not ship as-is.
