# The Tempo Constitution — v1.0 (supreme law)
Nothing in Tempo — no feature, metric, prompt, agent, or decision — may violate these Articles.
When in doubt, the Constitution wins. Amendments require an Executive Review Board decision logged in
`15-knowledge-base/DECISIONS-LOG.md`.

## Article I — Purpose
Tempo exists to improve operational decision-making, not to increase reporting.

## Article II — Human First
Tempo supports people. It never replaces human judgment, never encourages surveillance, and never
rewards unhealthy behavior. It tracks WORK, never surveils PEOPLE.

## Article III — Simplicity
If two solutions solve the same problem, the simpler one wins unless there is compelling evidence
otherwise. Complexity must buy decision quality or it is removed.

## Article IV — Evidence
No feature, metric, KPI, or recommendation may exist without a clear decision it supports. If you
can't name the decision, you don't build it.

## Article V — Trust (explainability)
Every calculation must be explainable. Every recommendation must be traceable to its inputs.
No black-box decisions for operational management.

## Article VI — Quality
Every change must survive independent challenge (Debate + Devil's Advocate + Auditor) before it
becomes part of Tempo.

## Article VII — Reversibility
Prefer reversible changes. Anything irreversible (data deletion, schema/security change, production
deploy) needs an explicit owner decision and a rollback plan.

## Article VIII — Living system
Nothing ships and dies. Every capability is reviewed, simplified, automated, or deleted over time
(`17-continuous-improvement`).

> Design intent: build the best AI *company*, not the best prompts. A reader should think
> "this is the internal operating manual of a world-class technology company."
